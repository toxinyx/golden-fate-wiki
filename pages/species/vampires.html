Basic Information

Vampires are also sometimes called 'Kage-oni (影鬼)', which translates to 'Shadow Demons'.

Naming Schemes

The naming schemes of Vampire culture are relatively simple as well as similar to the way Humans name themselves and their children. They tend to follow a two-name naming scheme of Given (First) Name + Surname/Family Name. Middle names are not unheard of, but they are not common amongst people born within Touwara. On occasion, a familial title may be relevant when a Vampire is introducing themselves, but this typically varies dependent on status and introduction.

Given names are given with intent by parents, holding either some kind of personal meaning or language meaning to how they intend to raise their child to be. They are rarely less than one or two syllables, making Vampiric names longer on average. Names may be commonly shared amongst family members to keep certain traditions or memories alive.

Surnames most often come from the male side of families. They are particularly important when it comes to families of higher status or nobility, accompanying significant titles bestowed to the most important people of the family itself. It is regarded as the widest form of recognition in Touwara, sometimes being described as 'sacred' to a person's identity. Abandoning or changing your surname (outside of marriage) is considered an unforgivable betrayal of the family and leads to people being outcasted or outsworn from the culture almost entirely.

Familial titles are only given to the highest families of Touwara, indicating significant importance of the culture. These 'important' families are reserved for historically-impactful individuals who are descendants of those who have had a strong impact or notable correlation to Vampiric History itself. The titles typically allow individuals to announce themselves to others; they begin with their given names and follow up with the given title. For example, 'Kuroyuki, Follower of Shadows' or 'Dalaga, the Blood Maiden'.

Characteristics (Appearance)

Over the course of time, Vampires have by many standards not changed much. There are many genetic factors that contribute to a vampire's physical appearance.

Height and Weight;
The average height for vampiric men is 5'9 - 5'10.
The average height for vampiric women is 5'6 - 5'7.
Vampires tend to be slimmer than the average person, and therefore may present as underweight or sickly.

Skin;
A vampire's skin is less pigmented than a normal person's. They can be of any colour (white, hispanic, black, etc), but their skin will be devoid of flush - a white individual will be paper-pale, while a black individual may appear an ashy gray as a result. Vampiric genes appear to remove the undertone of a person. It is also important to note that the skin of a vampire is much more sensitive than a human's. They bruise much easier, can cut themselves much easier, and would be wise to remain aware of their bodies at most times.

Hair;
Black is the dominant hair colour amongst vampires, closely followed by tinted white or gray. Other colours could happen, but is extremely rare; blonde being the rarest (typically only obtained when vampires attempt with another species).

When an individual is turned, their hair might begin to turn to a more prominant colour (such as black or white) over an extensive period of time. This is due to new hair follicles growing with the new vampiric genes, replacing the old genes of the race before. It may be a full transformation of colour, or may manifest in a splotch of colour instead. It is entirely dependant on how the turning takes effect, which is different on everyone's D.N.A.

Eyes;
Unlike popular belief, red eyes are not too common amongst Vampiric folk. Accompanying the sensitivity to light that Vampires experience, they are more predisposed to having light eyes such as blue, green, hazel, etc. Brown, dark brown and black eyes are, once more, possible, but genetically improbable. When turned, a person's eye colour will not change.
